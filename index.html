Over the past couple of months, I’ve found myself more and more inundated with university assignments and studying. Although I’m highly passionate and interested in the work I do as a computer science undergraduate, a lot of my time is often spent sitting in front of a computer in the library staring blankly at lines of code, an architectural diagram, or a word document. After all is said and done, I find myself exhausted on the train back home, usually long after the sun has set and Melbourne's night life starts to bustle.

Here, I stumble on the precipice between craving entertainment yet wanting something relaxing to wind down with. Unsurprisingly, a high-octane YouTube video or social media post didn’t hit the mark as it left me more drained than I already was. In fact, It didn’t occur to me until last year that a simple book fulfilled both those needs.

Thus started my unexpected fixation on reading!

Books I've Read

Dracula

Bram Stoker

Finished: Aug 2024

The Anchoress

Robyn Cadwallader

Finished: Dec 2024

The Alchemist

Paulo Coelho

Finished: March 2025

Although something I've heard time and time again, I didn't quite believe those who said that books held the power to immerse its readers in another world. I wanted to test this notion by starting off with a classic - Dracula. This turned out to be quite the rollercoaster than I initially anticipated. Despite being written nearly 130 years ago, its heartfelt characters and their camaraderie, the titular antagonist, as well as the haunting atmosphere made this read an unforgettable experience.

From there, I branched out to books I had read in the past but didn't quite enjoy to books recommended by friends. With each book, I find time passing by quickly than ever before, reducing my long train rides to what feels like a few minutes.

Currently Reading

Fahrenheit 451

Ray Bradbury

Started: May 2025

50%

By picking up reading as a hobby. I uncovered just how important it is in our current world to slow down and enjoy a slower paced activity. Although unfamiliar at first, I quickly found comfort sitting through a good book, no matter what genre it covers.

What started as a curiosity has quickly blossomed into a habit I genuinely value. I now look forward to sitting on those long rides to and from university so I can bury my head in the book I'm reading. More than anything, it has taught me how to enjoy the simpler things in life which is something up until last year, I didn't know I needed.
